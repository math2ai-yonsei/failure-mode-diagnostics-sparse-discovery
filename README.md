# Failure-Mode Diagnostics for Sparse Discovery of Nonlinear Dynamics under Low-Data Conditions

Reproducibility package for the manuscript of the same title, prepared for
submission to *Nonlinear Dynamics* (Springer).

This repository accompanies a study that treats sparse discovery of nonlinear
dynamics in the low-data regime as a **diagnose-before-augment** decision
problem. Rather than proposing a new estimator, it provides a failure-mode
taxonomy, an experiment-fixed diagnostic protocol, and empirical guidance on
when data augmentation, D-optimal trajectory selection, and library
reparameterization help, fail, or become harmful for ensemble sparse
identification of nonlinear dynamics (E-SINDy).

## Paper

- **Title:** Failure-Mode Diagnostics for Sparse Discovery of Nonlinear Dynamics under Low-Data Conditions
- **Author:** Jaehyun Lee, Department of Artificial Intelligence, Yonsei University, Seoul, Republic of Korea
- **Status:** Prepared for submission to *Nonlinear Dynamics* (Springer), 2026

## Reproducing the paper

`docs/reproduce_paper_results.md` lists, for every figure and table, the script
that regenerates it. The benchmark systems are:

- **Cart-Pole** — recall-fragility system
- **AEK self-balancing motorcycle** — precision-collapse stress-test (unresolved)
- **Lorenz-63** — precision collapse, coverage-adequate
- **Silverbox** — real engineering circuit benchmark
- **Lynx-Hare** — ecological benchmark (appendix)

Synthetic datasets (Cart-Pole, AEK, Lorenz-63) are generated by the simulators
in `src/simulators/`. The Silverbox and Lynx-Hare datasets are real measurements;
their provenance is documented in `docs/data_provenance.md`.

## Pool integrity

Each benchmark uses a fixed candidate trajectory pool identified by a SHA
fingerprint. The authoritative fingerprints are in `pool_sha_registry.md`. A
reviewer can regenerate a pool and confirm it matches the paper by comparing
its recomputed SHA against that table.
