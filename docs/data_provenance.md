# Data Provenance

This document records the origin of every dataset used in the paper and the
provenance of the approximate teacher models used for model-based augmentation.

## Real-measurement datasets

### Silverbox

- **System:** electronic Duffing oscillator circuit.
- **Source:** the Nonlinear System Identification Benchmarks
  (`https://www.nonlinearbenchmark.org`), distributed as `SNLS80mV.mat`.
- **Reference:** Wigren, T. & Schoukens, J. (2013), *Three free data sets for
  development and benchmarking in nonlinear system identification*.
- **Use:** the multisine `train_val` record provides the measurements from
  which the low-data training trajectories and the candidate pool are drawn.

**Teacher-model provenance (transparency note).** Model-based augmentation for
Silverbox uses an approximate teacher Duffing model. Its parameters are
*exploration-fixed*: they were determined once, by a least-squares fit on a
fixed segment of the full signal during a separate exploration step, and were
then frozen as a single-source-of-truth value before the diagnostic
experiments. They are **not** re-fitted at run time, and in particular are not
re-fitted from the three-trajectory training sets. Run-time re-fitting at the
low-data level is disabled because it is numerically unstable. The teacher uses
the free-oscillation (zero-input) form; this is stated as a methodological
limitation in the manuscript, since it restricts input-direction diversity in
the augmented pool.

### Lynx-Hare (appendix)

- **System:** predator-prey population record (Hudson Bay Company lynx and hare
  fur returns).
- **Content:** 21 unique annual observations spanning 1900-1920.
- **References:** MacLulich (1937); Elton & Nicholson (1942).
- **Use:** the Gaussian-mixture source is fitted on the 21 raw annual
  observations directly. Training windows are never flattened into the fitting
  set. This dataset is reported in the appendix only, with ecological caveats:
  it is a single historical realization at annual resolution, not a repeated
  controlled experiment.

## Synthetic datasets

The Cart-Pole, AEK self-balancing motorcycle, and Lorenz-63 datasets are
synthetic and are generated by the simulators in `src/simulators/`. Their
configurations are fixed in `configs/systems/`.

- **Cart-Pole** — recall-fragility benchmark.
- **AEK self-balancing motorcycle** — precision-collapse stress-test. The AEK
  trajectories are noise-free: state derivatives are obtained by Savitzky-Golay
  filtering of the clean simulated states. No artificial measurement noise is
  added.
- **Lorenz-63** — generated with the standard parameters; a single Rayleigh
  number is used consistently across splits (no mixing of multiple values).

## Preprocessing consistency

Across all five systems, every pool derivative and every training derivative is
computed with the Savitzky-Golay filter. Analytic derivatives are not used
anywhere. This consistency is required so that augmented trajectories share the
preprocessing of the training data; mismatched preprocessing introduces a
train-pool derivative confound that is especially decisive in the
coarse-resolution Lynx-Hare setting.
